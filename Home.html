The Arabian Peninsula, before the rise of Islam , was largely inhabited by tribal societies with a mix of Bedouins and other nomads . The region was filled with polytheistic people, with tribes worshiping a variety of gods and spirits, though some groups practiced Christianity, Judaism, or Early Islam. Mostly, the peninsula was important for trade, particularly along the land and water routes that connected it to the Arabian and Persian Empires.

>After the advent of Islam, the Arabian Peninsula underwent a profound transformation. Islam, founded by the Prophet Muhammad in Mecca, unified the previously nomad tribes under a single religious and political system. The region became the heart of a rapidly expanding Islamic empire, fostering advancements in governance, culture, and trade. Mecca and Medina became religious centers, main marketplace, and the spread of Islam led to the making of the Arabian society into a broader Islamic world, influencing regions far beyond the peninsula.
